Room 1: Learning Through Reflection and Experience

Artifact 1: Pecha Kucha

This video is a recording of the Pecha Kucha presentation I created for ADED565: Reading & Research. I randomly selected this article to read and reflect on for my Pecha Kucha presentation, and it was a great opportunity to practice summarizing and critiquing research in a concise format. It also provided me with the chance to reflect on my own learning and experience and realize how much I have grown as a learner and educator throughout the MAdEd. program to date. I also recognized that I too am a Feminist, and that my values and beliefs align with Feminist principles, which has influenced my approach to adult education. As a woman in a male dominated field (both professional and educational), I have experienced firsthand the challenges and barriers that exist for women in STEM.

Room 2: Making Technology Make Sense Through Hands-On Learning

Artifact 2: Technology Integration

This multimedia artifact created for ADED545: Critical Review of Literature in Adult Education explores the intersection of critical pedagogy and digital literacy through a conversational lens. Moving "Beyond the Browser," I examine how my professional work with TechPrep serves as a laboratory for applying the transformative theories of Paulo Freire and bell hooks to the digital divide.


The podcast centers on a fundamental question: Is digital education a tool for liberation or a modern site of banking-model oppression? Using the story of a learner named Dani, I trace the journey from "digital oppression" (toxic workplace environments and self-doubt) to "digital agency" (finding joy and confidence through learning).
